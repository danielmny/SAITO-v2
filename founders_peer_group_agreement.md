# FOUNDERS PEER GROUP — MEMBERSHIP AGREEMENT  Ref: FPG-MEM-AGR-001   Governing Law: Kingdom of Spain, Autonomous Community of Andalusia   Language: Executed in English; interpreted consistently with Spanish law.  ## PARTIES 1. The Organiser(s): The individual(s) or entity facilitating the Founders Peer Group, as identified at onboarding. 2. The Member: The individual named in the Membership Acceptance Form.  ## ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS - "Confidential Information" — any information disclosed by any Member or the Organiser in connection with the Group that a reasonable person would consider confidential. - "Group Sessions" — any meetings, calls, workshops, retreats, online forums, or other gatherings under the auspices of the Group. - "Ground Rules" — the behavioural norms communicated by the Organiser at the start of each Group Session.  ## ARTICLE 2 — CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON-DISCLOSURE 2.1 The Member shall keep strictly confidential all Confidential Information received in connection with the Group. This obligation survives termination indefinitely. 2.2 Permitted Disclosures: only with written consent, mandatory law/court order, or to legal counsel under equivalent obligations. 2.3 The Member shall protect Confidential Information with at least the same care applied to their own most sensitive information. 2.4 On termination or upon request, the Member shall promptly return or destroy all materials containing Confidential Information.  ## ARTICLE 3 — GROUND RULES AND CONDUCT 3.1 By attending any Group Session, the Member accepts the Ground Rules. 3.2 The Member agrees to: participate in good faith; not record sessions without consent; use Confidential Information solely for peer learning; treat all Members with dignity; promptly notify the Organiser of any suspected breach; engage constructively. 3.3 Material or repeated breach may result in immediate suspension or termination at the Organiser's sole discretion.  ## ARTICLE 4 — LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION 4.1 The Organiser's role is strictly limited to facilitating and moderating the Group. The Organiser makes no guarantee of any outcome or benefit from participation. 4.2 To the fullest extent permitted by Spanish law, the Organiser has no liability for any loss arising from participation, disclosure of Confidential Information, reliance on information shared, technical failures, or decisions regarding the Group's composition. 4.3 The Organiser cannot guarantee the conduct of other Members. 4.4 The Organiser may offer informal mediation as a courtesy only, with no obligation to intervene or reach resolution. 4.5 The Member shall indemnify the Organiser from all claims, damages, and costs arising from the Member's breach of this Agreement.  ## ARTICLE 5 — MEMBERSHIP TERM, RENEWAL, AND FEES 5.1 Flexibility Period: 3-month period from date of first attendance; Member may withdraw for any reason. 5.2 Annual Commitment: If not withdrawn during Flexibility Period, Member automatically enters a 12-month term. 5.3 Automatic Renewal: Renews for successive 12-month periods unless either Party gives notice. 5.4 Cancellation: From end of first Annual Term, 1 calendar month's written notice required. 5.5 Termination for Cause: Organiser may terminate immediately for material breach, harmful conduct, or insolvency. 5.6 Reassignment or Removal: Organiser reserves the absolute right to remove or reassign a Member at sole discretion. 5.7 No Refunds: All membership fees are strictly non-refundable. 5.8 On termination: right to attend ceases immediately; confidentiality obligations continue indefinitely.  ## ARTICLE 6 — DATA PROTECTION The Organiser processes the Member's personal data solely for administering membership, in accordance with GDPR and LOPDGDD.  ## ARTICLE 7 — GENERAL PROVISIONS 7.1 Governing Law: Spanish law, including the Código Civil, Código de Comercio, and applicable Andalusian legislation. 7.2 Jurisdiction: Courts of [City], Andalusia, Spain. 7.3 Entire Agreement: This Agreement, the Membership Acceptance Form, and the Ground Rules constitute the entire agreement. 7.4 Amendments: No amendment valid unless in writing and signed by both Parties. 7.5 No Waiver: Failure to enforce any provision does not constitute a waiver. 7.6 No Assignment: Member may not assign any right without Organiser's prior written consent.  ## ARTICLE 8 — SEVERABILITY If any provision is found invalid, it shall be severed to the minimum extent necessary and all remaining provisions continue in full force.  ## ARTICLE 9 — ACKNOWLEDGEMENT AND EXECUTION By signing (or accepting electronically), the Member confirms they have read and understood this Agreement and agree to be bound by all its terms.  --- Version 1.2 — Drafted for compliance with Spanish Civil Law, GDPR/LOPDGDD, and applicable Andalusian regional regulation.
