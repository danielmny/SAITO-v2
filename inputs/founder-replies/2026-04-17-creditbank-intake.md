## What should we call this project?

CreditBank

## What project key should SAITO use for it?

CreditBank

## What kind of project is it?

Software project with an API plus front-end and back-end surfaces.

## What stage is it in right now?

IDEA

## Give me the one-line summary.

CreditBank is a transaction system that uses credits to pay for transactions between users and agents, including services, data, processing, and transactions between agents.

## What is the main objective right now?

Explore competition, determine which architectures can work, and validate whether the problem, market, and business model are real enough to pursue.

## What core problem does it solve?

Users need to pay for access and services from web apps and agents. Agents need work done by other agents and should be able to pay for that work. CreditBank offers a transaction system for users to buy credits to pay for apps and agents, and then lets paid agents use those credits to pay other agents or services inside the same circular economy.

## Who feels this problem most directly?

Users of these apps.

## Why does this matter now?

This is becoming a real need and there does not appear to be a similar solution right now.

## Who is the primary ICP?

Owners or users of web apps that offer services directly or through agents, plus owners of aggregators of web apps or agents.

## What observable traits define that ICP?

Undetermined at the moment. This needs research and sharper ICP definition.

## Who are the likely early adopters?

The founder first, then adjacent builders with similar needs once the wedge is clearer.

## What is the product wedge?

Undetermined at the moment. This needs research and wedge definition.

## What is the core workflow?

User buys credits, spends credits on an app or agent service, the receiving app or agent is credited, that agent can then spend credits on other agents or services, and CreditBank records, routes, settles, and monetizes the flow through commissions on credit purchase, transactions, and possibly holding balances.

## Why does this win over alternatives?

There do not seem to be direct alternatives yet. This needs research to confirm the competitive set and sharpen the differentiation.

## What evidence do we already have?

Only the founder's own need so far. This needs external research and validation.

## Which assumptions still need testing?

All of them: problem validation, pain points, frequency, intensity, urgency, effectiveness, willingness to adopt, unit economics, and whether the circular credit model is understandable and attractive.

## What are the next proof steps?

TBD after research. Likely market research, competitive mapping, ICP clarification, architecture exploration, and early validation interviews.

## What is the go-to-market plan?

Needs research by SAITO and its agents.

## How should we position it?

Potentially as a simpler Stripe-like system for a different purpose: a very easy-to-use user-facing system plus a very easy-to-use programmatic API for conducting transactions and monetizing services, agent processing, and agent output.

## What are the key risks?

The economics may not work, the system may be too complex, security may fail, the market may not be there, users may not see the need, or competitors may already be more advanced. These all need research and validation.

## What is the revenue model?

Revenue from credit sales, commissions on transactions, and potentially anonymized transaction data, subject to research and validation.
